## 1. The trigger — a misidentified paper

The session began when the student dropped **arXiv:2604.02486**, believing it was PAPO and worried it might scoop the project.

It is **not PAPO**. It is **"VLMs Need Words: Vision Language Models Ignore Visual Detail In Favor of Semantic Anchors"** (Shahgir, Chen, Fu, Shayegani, Abu-Ghazaleh, Kementchedjhieva, Dong; UC Riverside + MBZUAI; submitted 2 Apr 2026). PAPO is the *separate* **arXiv:2507.06448** (Wang et al., ICLR 2026).

Why the mix-up mattered rather than being a footnote: 2604.02486 argues a *thesis adjacent to ours* — that VLMs hold visual information internally but discard it at output (explained, in their telling, by "nameability"/"semantic anchors"). On first read it looks like a scoop. Establishing precisely what it does and does *not* do (full characterization in `06` §1 and `07` cluster A) was the thing that forced the larger question: **if a closely-adjacent paper now exists, is our phenomenon still novel at all?** That question is what kicked off the refresh. The honest answer turned out to be "no, the phenomenon is no longer novel — but the method is," which reshaped the entire framing of the project.

## 2. Three parallel research agents → docs 06 / 07 / 08

To answer the novelty question rigorously rather than by gut, three research agents were dispatched in parallel:

1. **Scoop-check** — does 2604.02486 (and anything else recent) actually claim our specific result? Output folded into `06` §1–2 and `07` cluster A.
2. **Frontier survey** — what has the 2025–2026 literature established about "encoded-but-not-expressed" since the original 2026-05-17 audit (`01`)? Output is `07_literature_survey_full.md` (clusters A–G, with a verification ledger — every arXiv ID was fetched and confirmed this session unless explicitly flagged UNVERIFIED).
3. **Methodology design** — given the refreshed landscape, what is the defensible method and what should change? Output is `08_design_alternatives_and_rationale.md` (decisions D1–D7, each as Options → Chosen → Why → Revisit-if).

Their consolidated conclusions (locked in `06_literature_refresh_and_methodology_lock.md`):

- **(a) The phenomenon is already established; our novelty is the method + framing, not the discovery.** The key prior is **Fu et al. 2025, arXiv:2506.08008** ("Hidden in plain sight"), plus 2604.02486 and several EMNLP'25 Findings papers (`07` cluster A). So the contribution must be the *combination no one has done*: rigorous trained-probe + controls on the **wrong-output contrastive subset**, on **BLINK**, with **Qwen2.5-VL**, with construct-validity controls and a causal internal intervention, framed as a direct **PAPO rebuttal** (the exact clause-set is in `06` §2.2). We must *cite the phenomenon as prior* and not claim to have found it.
- **(b) Construct validity is the crux.** A probe predicting the gold letter from hidden states could be reading visual grounding, or question/option-structure priors, or label artifacts — and *no gate in the old plan separated these*. This is the failure mode the methodology literature (Ravichander 2005.00719; Belinkov 2102.12452) calls fatal. See `08` D1.
- **(c) The causal plan was revised away from INLP-amnesic-probing to activation patching.** The INLP critique — **Kumar, Tan & Sharma 2022, arXiv:2207.04153** (NeurIPS 2022) — proves post-hoc removal can't fully remove a concept and may destroy collateral features, so an INLP accuracy-drop is *ambiguous* and unsafe as a load-bearing causal claim. Activation patching (Meng 2202.05262; Geiger interchange-intervention logic) answers "does the model *use* this state?" directly. If erasure is ever wanted, LEACE (2306.03819) replaces INLP, not INLP itself. See `08` D3 and `06` §4.
- **(d) The most likely and most valuable outcome is the middle case — "encoded but not used"** (the knowledge–action gap), anchored by **arXiv:2603.18353** ("Interpretability without actionability": 98.2% probe AUROC vs only 45.1% intervention success on Qwen2.5-7B). This made us pre-register a *trichotomy* of outcomes rather than a binary, so we cannot result-shop (`08` D6, `06` §5).

Two methodology choices were *locked* here that bear on the rest of the day: **construct validity via a counterfactual/visual ablation** (not just blank-image — see note in §5 on why the noise-image variant was the one actually run), and **difference-in-means (DiM) as the primary probe** rather than logistic regression (Marks & Tegmark 2310.06824; rationale in `08` D2 — DiM directions are more causally implicated and carry into the patching stage).

## 3. The code audit — the headline rested on a winner's curse

With the method under review, we audited the existing pipeline (`pilot/baselines.py`) and found the Gate 1 result was statistically unsafe:

- `baselines.py` selected the **best of 36 layers by wrong-subset accuracy** on a **single 25% test split** of **only 4–25 wrong-test samples per task**, then reported *that selected layer's* CI **uncorrected**.
- This is a textbook selection bias (winner's curse): maximizing over 36 noisy estimates and then reporting the max as if it were unbiased inflates the apparent peak. It was already flagged as a worry in `05_open_questions.md` (Q2/Q3) but had not been corrected.
- Concretely, the two "survivors" were thin: Object_Localization "passed" on ~15 wrong-test samples, Forensic_Detection on ~23. Real-looking, but fragile.

This audit is *why* a brand-new gate — **Gate 0** — was inserted *before* the previously-planned Gate 2. Gate 0 is not a new experiment; it is the old Gate 1 re-estimated with honest statistics. (`06` §7 "Statistics & scale"; `08` D4 layer-selection.)

## 4. Gate 0 — honest re-analysis under nested cross-validation

**Code**: `pilot/gate0_cv.py` (its self-test passes: it recovers a planted signal layer and asserts no train/test leakage). **Output**: `pilot/outputs/gate0_cv_summary.json`. **Full write-up**: `03_methodology_log.md` §"Gate 0".

**Design** (the three fixes, all chosen *before* looking at results):
- **Layer selection moved inside the inner CV folds** of nested 5-fold CV — the reported layer is no longer chosen on the data it is scored on.
- **Outer folds pooled** so that every sample is predicted exactly once → the wrong-subset CI is computed over the **full** 20–111 wrong samples per task, not a 4–25 sliver.
- **DiM as the primary probe** (the D2 decision), with logreg reported alongside for comparability.

**Result — the headline did NOT survive.** Both former survivors *failed* honest CV:

- **Forensic_Detection**: wrong-acc 0.182, CI [0.13, 0.30], *below* majority 0.265; selectivity −0.046.
- **Object_Localization**: wrong-acc 0.291, CI [0.16, 0.39], *below* majority 0.566; selectivity −0.200.

The inner-CV layer-selection histograms confirmed the diagnosis directly: Object_Localization's "peak" scattered across L33/31/32/34/2/27/12/0/28/26/10 — there was **no stable peak**. L21 was a single-split artifact. (See the `selected_layer_hist` fields in `gate0_cv_summary.json`.) **The "2/14 robust tasks" headline was retracted.**

**The diagnostic that replaced it — and flipped the candidates.** A transient gold-vs-output diagnostic (`gate0_diag`) asked, on the wrong-output subset: does the DiM prediction match the **gold** answer (our H1: "knows but says wrong") or the **model's own parsed wrong output** (PAPO-consistent: the internal state matches the wrong answer)? The result (full table in `03` §Gate 0):

- **Probe reads the model's own WRONG output for 9/14 tasks** (Counting, Relative_Depth, Spatial_Relation, Visual_Similarity at ~1.00; plus Forensic_Detection, Object_Localization, Relative_Reflectance, Semantic_Correspondence, Visual_Correspondence). **This is PAPO-consistent** — there is no hidden correct grounding to recover; the internal state agrees with the wrong answer.
- **Probe reads GOLD (the H1 signal) for**: **Jigsaw (1.00)**, **Multi-view_Reasoning (0.91)**, **Art_Style (0.58, weak)**.

Two things to preserve about the interpretation here, because they are easy to forget later:
1. The high "→output" fractions are **good news for the instrument** — they show the pre-generation hidden state strongly encodes the answer the model is about to emit, so the probe machinery is sound. The scientific signal is in *which tasks ALSO recover gold despite a wrong output*.
2. The candidates **flipped**. The morning's survivors (Forensic, Object_Localization) turned out to be PAPO-consistent (they read output). The morning's *excluded* tasks (Jigsaw, Multi-view) became the H1 candidates. And this *vindicated* the 2026-05-18 flag that the `dim_gap > 0.05` criterion was too strict (`05` Q1): Jigsaw/Multi-view had "failed" Gate 1 only because logreg couldn't beat DiM — making DiM primary (D2) surfaced them. The IDs in `gate0_cv_summary.json` show `passes_gate0: true` for Jigsaw, Multi-view_Reasoning, and IQ_Test; IQ_Test passed the coarse selectivity/CI flags but the gold-vs-output diagnostic put it in "mixed/noise" (→gold 0.40, →neither 0.55), so it was not carried forward as an H1 candidate.

**Immediate caution recorded at this point**: Jigsaw and Multi-view are *binary* tasks hitting ~1.0 gold-recovery — suspiciously clean. A 1.00 DiM is exactly what a **non-visual question/format artifact** would also produce. So Gate 3 was promoted from "confirmatory" to **load-bearing**: it now had to decide whether the gold signal is genuinely *visual* or an artifact (`03` §Gate 0 decision point 3).

## 5. Gate 3 — construct validity via noise-image ablation

**Code**: `pilot/run_inference_noise.py` (re-ran inference on the same samples with each image replaced by uniform RGB noise of identical size/count — text prompt and vision-token structure held identical, only image *content* changed; 400 samples, 0 errors, 833 s on Mac MPS) + `pilot/gate3_construct.py` (compared real-image vs noise-image DiM nested-CV gold-recovery on the wrong-output subset). **Output**: `pilot/outputs/gate3_construct_summary.json`. **Full write-up**: `03_methodology_log.md` §"Gate 3".

**Why noise-image rather than the counterfactual image-swap that `06`/`08` D1 named as *primary*.** This is a connective detail worth preserving: the locked design (`08` D1) chose counterfactual image-swap as primary because it freezes every linguistic confound while varying visual content. But the two surviving tasks (Jigsaw, Multi-view) **embed their answer options *in the image*** — so a clean question-fixed image-swap (same question, different image whose correct answer is a different option letter) is not minable from the existing runs. The noise-image ablation is the practical realization of the same construct-validity logic for these specific tasks: hold the text and vision-token structure fixed, destroy only the visual *content*, and ask whether the gold signal collapses. This is the `08` D1 "Revisit if" branch (counterfactual pairs unminable → fall back to noise-image control) being exercised. The logic is identical to the original `04_next_step_vision_ablation.md` idea, *re-targeted to the correct tasks per Gate 0*.

**Result** (wrong-output subset, 5-seed mean; values from `gate3_construct_summary.json`):

| task | majority | real gold-acc (wrong) | noise gold-acc (wrong) | visual attribution | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Jigsaw** | 0.527 | **0.99** CI[1.00,1.00] | 0.588 CI[0.41,0.64] | **+0.402** | VISUAL — H1 holds |
| **Multi-view_Reasoning** | 0.556 | **0.93** CI[0.84,0.97] | 0.511 CI[0.40,0.63] | **+0.423** | VISUAL — H1 holds |
| Art_Style | 0.530 | 0.519 CI[0.46,0.70] | 0.396 CI[0.32,0.56] | +0.123 | inconclusive → **dropped** |

**Interpretation:**
- **Jigsaw and Multi-view_Reasoning: H1 CONFIRMED and construct-valid.** Real-image gold-recovery (0.93–0.99) **collapses to chance (≈ majority) under noise**. A format/text artifact would have *survived* the noise swap, so the binary-artifact worry from Gate 0 is **ruled out**: the residual stream linearly encodes the *correct* answer, derived *from the image*, on examples where the model emits the wrong answer. For these two tasks, Qwen2.5-VL-3B "sees but cannot say."
- **Art_Style: dropped.** Its real gold-recovery (0.519) was never clearly above majority (CI includes 0.530); it was weak from the start (it was the "(weak)" gold-reader in Gate 0). Not an H1 task.
- Sanity check observed: under noise the model collapsed to a degenerate prior (e.g., always outputting one letter) — exactly what you expect when no visual information is present.

**Conclusion and honest framing (the most important thing to carry into the paper):** H1 ("sees but cannot say") is **confirmed and construct-valid, but TASK-SPECIFIC** — 2 of 14 tasks (Jigsaw, Multi-view_Reasoning), both binary. The other **9 tasks are PAPO-consistent**. So, *run rigorously, the project currently supports PAPO more than it refutes it*; H1 holds narrowly. This is not a setback to hide — it is the result, and it is publishable as the trichotomy (`08` D6). Scope limits to carry forward: (1) only 2/14 → task-specific, not a general property; (2) both binary (the dissociation is easiest to detect there); (3) this is decodability + construct validity → **correlational**; it does **not** yet show the model *uses* the encoded direction; (4) 3B only — generality (7B, other architectures) untested.

## 6. Next step set — Gate 4 (activation patching)

The decodability and construct-validity legs are done (and correlational). The remaining question is causal: **does the model *use* the encoded direction, or merely encode it?** That is Gate 4 (`06` §6 step G4; `08` D3).

**Plan**: activation patching on the *2 confirmed tasks only* (Jigsaw, Multi-view_Reasoning). Patch a correct-run hidden state (or the DiM component) at the peak layer into a wrong-run forward pass; measure flip-to-gold **versus a random-direction patch control** (the patching analogue of selectivity). VLM precedent: 2509.22674 flips ~23% of wrong outputs by patching.

**Pre-registered risk**: the knowledge–action gap (**arXiv:2603.18353**) — the likely outcome is "encoded but only *partially* used," not a clean causal YES. We commit to reporting that as the headline if it occurs (it is the sharpest result: partially refutes PAPO on representation, partially vindicates it on usage).

**The 3B-vs-7B decision point (lands at/just before G4)** (`06` §8; `08` D5): run the **3B causal pilot now** (cheap, runs on the Mac, uses existing infra) and **reserve 7B for the final causal + generality claim** (needs GPU budget — confirm with advisor, `05` Q7). Rationale: G0/G3 were free on existing 3B data and have done their job; but patching flip-rates at N≈57–70 will have wide CIs, so a 3B null would be *underpowered, not negative* — the 7B run is justified independently (the spec already targets 7B; HALP's Qwen2.5-VL probe numbers are 7B; 3B survivors are thin) and doubles as the generality check. We explicitly do **not** add more tasks (2/14 is a real result, not a power problem) and do **not** fabricate a power number.

- **Demoted INLP; adopted activation patching as the primary causal test** (INLP critique: Kumar et al. 2022, 2207.04153); LEACE if erasure is ever needed (`08` D3, `06` §4).
